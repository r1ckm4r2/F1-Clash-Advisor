# Boost Reference

Boosts are consumable items used during races to raise specific stats. Players should choose boosts that align with the track's primary stat needs — the same priority logic as components and drivers.

**Stats key:**
- **OVT** – Overtaking | **DEF** – Defending | **RST** – Race Start | **TYR** – Tyre Use
- **SPD** – Speed | **CRN** – Cornering | **PWR** – Power Unit
- **PIT** – Pit Time reduction | **IMP** – Impact (Battery overtake boost)
- **TOT** – Total stat points added
- **-** = no bonus to that stat

## Boost Selection Logic

Match boosts to the track's primary stat needs, same as components and drivers:
- **Speed tracks** → boosts with high SPD (Champion, Dead Fast, Eclipse, Full Send, Skull, Taurus)
- **Cornering tracks** → boosts with high CRN (Hook, Merlion, Movember, Timeless)
- **Power Unit tracks** → boosts with high PWR (Butterfly, Dead Fast, Rookie Reload, Samba, Taurus)
- **Overtaking** → boosts with high OVT (Cranberry, Eternal Flame, Feral, Ghoulfuel, Unstoppable)
- **Defending** → boosts with high DEF (Genesis, Gladiator, Handlebar, Rainbow, Vice)
- **Race Start** → boosts with high RST (Confetti, Herald, Launch Pad, Schooner, Street Shark, Surf)
- **Tyre management** → boosts with high TYR (Frost, Pretzel, Tsar)
- **Pit Time** → boosts with high PIT (Crown, Eggcepton, Lumberjack, Tune-In)

When a player has multiple boosts available, recommend the ones that cover the track's top 1–2 stat priorities with the highest combined value.
